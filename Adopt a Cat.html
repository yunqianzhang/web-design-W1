How to Rehome From Us

We work really hard to find each adopter the right cat. Sadly, many of the cats in our care have been victims of cruelty which means they often require a period of rehabilitation before they are ready to be rehomed. When our volunteers believe they are ready they will observe their behaviour and give them a health check which helps us match their needs to a suitable person or family.

Special Homes for Special Cats

Many of the cats in our care have had a tough time, so it’s very important that their next home is the right home and a home for life. That’s why we ask anyone who’s thinking of rehoming from us to fill in an application form before being interviewed with one of our rescue centre volunteers. We find this is the best way to find out more about you and help you to choose the right cat. We really believe there is a home to suit every cat in our care. But, sometimes it just takes a little time to find our feline friends the perfect home.

Home Visits

We carry out a home-visit and in some cases suggest minor changes that help ensure your home is as cat friendly as possible. We’ll also call or visit after your cat has moved in to make sure you and your new cat are adjusting well to your new life together.

Neutering

All cats that are over the age of 6 months are neutered before rehoming. If adopting a cat that is younger than 6 months, Tabby Cats Rescue will provide a voucher for half price neutering at a York-based veterinarian.

Fee

A fee of £50 per cat is charged upon rehoming a cat which contributes to the running costs of Tabby Cat Rescue.
