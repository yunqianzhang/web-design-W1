Jazzy, Joey, Jenny and Jim

Jazzy, Joey, Jenny and Jim are our family of 4 adorable ginger kittens. The kittens were found in a box at the side of the road when they were just 3 weeks old. They are now 8 weeks old and looking for new families. These kittens have been through a lot in their short lives and must be rehomed in pairs. They are playful and excitable and enjoy lots of attention.

Milly

Milly came to us when her owner passed away suddenly. Milly is about 8 years old. She loves to be outdoors and must be rehomed to a family where there is space for her to roam and explore. Milly has lived with other animals before and can be rehomed into a family with other pets. Milly has been with us for 6 months now and desperately wants a new home.

Fonzie

Fonzie is a very cool character. He’s been at Tabby Cats for over a year now, as he arrived in a sad and neglected condition. His previous owners had not taken care of him properly and his gorgeous fur was all matted and dirty and full of nasty bugs. It’s taken a lot of hard work from the volunteers, but Fonzie is now ready for rehoming. Fonzie is a bit shy and timid until he gets to know you, but once he feels comfortable around someone he is a very affectionate little dude. Fonzie requires a home where he is the only pet, and there are no young children. His fur needs regular grooming to keep in its current healthy state.

Bill (the ginger cat) and Ben (the tabby)

Bill (the ginger cat) and Ben (the tabby) are a pair of 5 year old cats who have lived together their whole lives. They aren’t brothers, but they may as well be. They came to Tabby Cats when their family emigrated to Australia and decided they couldn’t take the cats with them. Given they are very close, Bill and Ben must be rehomed together. In many ways Bill and Ben are opposites. Bill is not the cleverest cat we’ve had in Tabby Cats rescue, but he is a sweetheart and will always enjoy a cuddle. Ben, on the other hand, is incredibly smart and savvy. He knows what he wants (usually more food), and he knows how to get it (by pretending he hasn’t just been fed). Bill and Ben are used to being allowed to play inside and out so need to be in a quiet area with a cat flap. Bill and Ben are used to young children and dogs.

Cindy

How to Rehome From Us

We work really hard to find each adopter the right cat. Sadly, many of the cats in our care have been victims of cruelty which means they often require a period of rehabilitation before they are ready to be rehomed. When our volunteers believe they are ready they will observe their behaviour and give them a health check which helps us match their needs to a suitable person or family.

Special Homes for Special Cats

Many of the cats in our care have had a tough time, so it’s very important that their next home is the right home and a home for life. That’s why we ask anyone who’s thinking of rehoming from us to fill in an application form before being interviewed with one of our rescue centre volunteers. We find this is the best way to find out more about you and help you to choose the right cat. We really believe there is a home to suit every cat in our care. But, sometimes it just takes a little time to find our feline friends the perfect home.

Home Visits

We carry out a home-visit and in some cases suggest minor changes that help ensure your home is as cat friendly as possible. We’ll also call or visit after your cat has moved in to make sure you and your new cat are adjusting well to your new life together.

Neutering

All cats that are over the age of 6 months are neutered before rehoming. If adopting a cat that is younger than 6 months, Tabby Cats Rescue will provide a voucher for half price neutering at a York-based veterinarian.

Fee

A fee of £50 per cat is charged upon rehoming a cat which contributes to the running costs of Tabby Cat Rescue.
